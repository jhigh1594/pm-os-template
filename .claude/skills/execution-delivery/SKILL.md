3. **Assess Capacity**
   - Who's available? (PTO, other commitments)
   - What's our realistic capacity?
   - Should we carry over incomplete work?

#### **Unblocking (Critical Daily Work)**

**When you tell me about a blocker, I'll help:**

**1. Categorize the Block:**
- **Technical:** Architecture decision, technical unknown, complex bug
- **Resource:** Capacity constraint, skill gap, dependency on another team
- **Decision:** Unclear requirements, conflicting priorities, stakeholder decision needed
- **External:** Vendor issue, legal/compliance, budget approval

**2. Assess Urgency:**
- **Critical** (Blocks immediate work): Resolve within 24 hours
- **High** (Blocks upcoming work): Resolve within 2-3 days
- **Medium** (Blocks future work): Resolve within 1-2 weeks
- **Low** (Nice to resolve): Address when capacity allows

**3. Develop Unblocking Plan:**

**For Technical Blocks:**
- Convene technical spike meeting
- Make architecture decision (use /decide if needed)
- Prototype to reduce unknowns
- Find alternative approach

**For Resource Blocks:**
- Reprioritize other work
- Bring in additional resources
- Adjust scope to fit capacity
- Escalate for headcount

**For Decision Blocks:**
- Clarify requirements with stakeholders
- Apply decision framework (one-way vs two-way door)
- Make call with 70% certainty if reversible
- Escalate to decision owner if one-way door

**For External Blocks:**
- Find temporary workaround
- Escalate through appropriate channels
- Adjust timeline to accommodate
- Identify alternative path

**4. Track Until Resolved:**
- Who's working on unblocking?
- Expected resolution date?
- Follow-up plan if not resolved?

#### **Scope Management**

**Watch for scope creep:**
- New requirements added mid-sprint?
- Stories growing in complexity?
- "Just one more thing" syndrome?

**Scope management tactics:**
- **Defer to next sprint:** Most common, defer new work
- **Swap stories:** If new work is critical, remove something else
- **Split story:** Keep must-have in sprint, defer nice-to-have
- **Say no:** Firmly but kindly reject scope creep

**Trade-off discussions:**
- Scope vs. Quality: Can we reduce scope to maintain quality?
- Scope vs. Timeline: Can we push date to deliver full scope?
- Quality vs. Timeline: Can we ship lower quality to hit date? (Usually NO)

### **Phase 4: Retrospectives & Continuous Improvement** (End of sprint/quarter)

**Goal:** Learn from execution and improve process.

**Sprint Retrospective (30-45 min at end of each sprint):**

**Format: Start / Stop / Continue**

**Start (What should we start doing?):**
- New practices to try
- Tools to adopt
- Process improvements

**Stop (What should we stop doing?):**
- Wasteful activities
- Ineffective meetings
- Processes that slow us down

**Continue (What's working well?):**
- Effective practices
- Useful rituals
- Things to keep doing

**Alternative Formats:**
- **4Ls:** Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For
- **Mad/Sad/Glad:** What made you mad/sad/glad this sprint?
- **Sailboat:** What's propelling us (wind)? What's holding us back (anchors)?

#### **Velocity Analysis**

**Track over time:**
- Average velocity (rolling 3-sprint average)
- Velocity trend (up/stable/down)
- Completion rate (% of committed work finished)
- Carry-over rate (% of work carried to next sprint)

**If velocity is declining:**
- Are we over-committing?
- Is quality suffering (tech debt accumulating)?
- Are there persistent blockers?
- Is team capacity changing?
- Action: Reduce commitment, address root causes

**If velocity is increasing:**
- Are we getting better at estimation?
- Is team improving collaboration?
- Are we cutting corners? (check quality metrics)
- Action: Celebrate, but watch quality

**If velocity is volatile:**
- Are estimates accurate?
- Are there external disruptions?
- Is scope stable?
- Action: Improve estimation, stabilize scope

#### **Quality Metrics Review**

**Track:**
- Bug rate (bugs reported / features shipped)
- Production incidents (count, severity)
- Tech debt (% of sprint on tech debt)
- Cycle time (idea â†’ shipped)

**Quality declining signals:**
- Increasing bug rate
- More production incidents
- Growing tech debt
- Slower cycle time

**Action:** Allocate % of sprint to quality (tech debt, refactoring, bug fixes)

#### **Process Improvements**

**What to improve:**
- Planning rituals (more/less time, different format)
- Communication cadence (daily standups working? stakeholder updates clear?)
- Tooling (better tracking, automation opportunities)
- Team skills (training, pairing, knowledge sharing)

**Commit to 1-2 improvements per sprint:**
- Keep it small and actionable
- Assign owner and timeline
- Review impact in next retrospective

**Deliverable:** Actionable improvements, updated execution process
