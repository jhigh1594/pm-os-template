## The Competitive Analysis Process (4 Phases)

### **Phase 1: Competitor Selection & Research Design** (Session 1, 30-45 min)

**Goal:** Define which competitors to analyze and how.

**Start by asking me:**
1. **Which competitor(s) should we analyze?** (Name, why them, why now)
2. **What type of competitor are they?**
   - Direct (same customer, same solution)
   - Indirect (same customer, different solution)
   - Emerging (new entrant, disruptor)
   - Substitute (different approach to same job)
3. **What decisions will this inform?** (Product strategy, positioning, pricing, GTM)
4. **How deep should we go?** (Quick assessment vs. comprehensive deep-dive)
5. **What's our timeline?** (Days, weeks, months)

**Then help me:**

#### **Prioritize Competitors**

For each competitor, assess:
- **Strategic Threat Level:** How big a threat? (High / Medium / Low)
- **Market Overlap:** How much do we directly compete? (%)
- **Learning Value:** What can we learn from them? (High / Medium / Low)

This determines how much time/resources to invest.

#### **Define Research Questions**

Transform vague goals into specific intelligence questions:
- ‚ùå Vague: "Understand Competitor X"
- ‚úÖ Specific: "What's Competitor X's product roadmap for next 6 months? What customer segments are they targeting? How do they position against us?"

#### **Research Plan**

**Information sources to leverage:**
- Product (trials, demos, documentation)
- Customers (reviews, case studies, interviews)
- Company (funding, hiring, strategy)
- GTM (pricing, marketing, sales)
- Strategic (positioning, moats, vulnerabilities)

**Methods:**
- Desk research (website, documentation, reviews)
- Product trials (hands-on experience)
- Customer intelligence (what customers say)
- Web research (fetch pricing pages, blog posts, job listings)
- File analysis (review existing competitive intel)

**Deliverable:** Competitor research plan with questions, sources, and timeline

### **Phase 3: Strategic Analysis** (After intelligence gathering, 60-90 min)

**Goal:** Synthesize intelligence into strategic insights.

#### **3a. SWOT Analysis**

For each major competitor:

**Strengths:**
- What they do really well
- Their competitive advantages
- What customers love

**Weaknesses:**
- Where they struggle
- Customer complaints
- Product/market gaps

**Opportunities (for us):**
- Where we can attack their weaknesses
- Unmet needs in their customer base
- Market shifts they're not prepared for

**Threats (to us):**
- Where they're strong and we're weak
- Roadmap signals indicating future competition
- Strategic moves that could hurt us

#### **3c. Porter's Five Forces**

Market-level competitive dynamics:

**1. Competitive Rivalry:**
- How intense is competition? (Low / Medium / High)
- How many significant competitors?
- Rate of industry growth?

**2. Threat of New Entrants:**
- How easy for new competitors to enter? (Barriers?)
- Capital requirements
- Regulatory barriers
- Brand/network effects as barriers

**3. Bargaining Power of Suppliers:**
- How much leverage do suppliers have?
- Switching costs for suppliers
- Importance to suppliers

**4. Bargaining Power of Buyers:**
- How much leverage do customers have?
- Switching costs for customers
- Price sensitivity

**5. Threat of Substitutes:**
- What alternative solutions exist?
- How viable are substitutes?
- Switching costs to substitutes

**Industry Attractiveness:** Low / Medium / High

#### **3d. Moat Analysis (7 Powers)**

For each major competitor, assess their defensibility:

**1. Scale Economies:** Cost advantages from size? (Weak / Moderate / Strong)
**2. Network Effects:** Product gets better with more users? (Weak / Moderate / Strong)
**3. Counter-Positioning:** Unique positioning hard to copy? (Yes / No)
**4. Switching Costs:** Hard for customers to leave? (Weak / Moderate / Strong)
**5. Branding:** Strong brand equity? (Weak / Moderate / Strong)
**6. Cornered Resource:** Exclusive access to valuable asset? (Yes / No, what?)
**7. Process Power:** Operational advantages from experience? (Weak / Moderate / Strong)

**Overall Moat Strength:** Weak / Moderate / Strong / Very Strong

### **Phase 4: Strategic Recommendations & Battlecards** (Final session, 45-60 min)

**Goal:** Translate analysis into actionable strategy and sales enablement.

#### **Strategic Recommendations**

**1. Product Strategy:**
- **Invest:** Where to widen our lead or build moats
- **Close gaps:** Critical features we need to match
- **Avoid:** What NOT to build (their game, not ours)

**2. Positioning Strategy:**
- **Our differentiation:** How we position against them
- **Messaging:** What resonates vs. their messaging
- **Counter-positioning:** How we frame the market differently

**3. Pricing Strategy:**
- **Comparison:** How our pricing stacks up
- **Adjustments:** Should we change pricing/packaging?
- **Response:** How to handle price-based competition

**4. Sales Strategy:**
- **Where we win:** Scenarios to pursue aggressively
- **Where we lose:** Scenarios to avoid or reposition
- **Handling objections:** How to address their strengths

**5. Threat Assessment:**
- **Urgency:** How immediate is the competitive threat? (High / Medium / Low)
- **Response plan:** 6-12 month competitive strategy
