# Mental Models for Strategic Thinking

This catalog provides the complete set of mental models available for strategic analysis. During strategic thinking sessions, 3-5 models will be selected based on the specific decision context.

---

## ROI & Economics Models

### **Expected Value**
**Definition:** Probability × Impact = Expected Value

**When to apply:** Evaluating strategic options under uncertainty
**Key questions:**
- What's the probability this succeeds?
- What's the magnitude of outcome if it does?
- How does this option's expected value compare to alternatives?

**Related:** Decision trees, probability-weighted scenarios

---

### **Time Value of Shipping**
**Definition:** The cost of waiting includes opportunity cost, market timing risk, and learning delay

**When to apply:** Deciding between "ship now" vs "ship later"
**Key questions:**
- What do we learn by shipping earlier?
- What's the opportunity cost of waiting?
- How does market timing affect success probability?

**Related:** First-mover advantage, network effects

---

### **Unit Economics**
**Definition:** Customer-level economics (CAC, LTV, payback period)

**When to apply:** Business model validation, scaling decisions
**Key questions:**
- What's the cost to acquire a customer?
- What's the lifetime value of a customer?
- How long to payback acquisition cost?

**Related:** ROI, contribution margin, economies of scale

---

### **ROI (Return on Investment)**
**Definition:** (Gain from Investment - Cost of Investment) / Cost of Investment

**When to apply:** Comparing investment options across different scales
**Key questions:**
- Which option delivers most value per investment?
- What's the payback period?
- Are we optimizing for short-term or long-term ROI?

**Related:** Expected value, opportunity cost

---

## Time & Strategy Models

### **Time Horizon**
**Definition:** The temporal lens through which you evaluate strategic options (short-term vs long-term)

**When to apply:** Balancing immediate needs with future positioning
**Key questions:**
- Are we optimizing for this quarter or next year?
- What's the long-term strategic position?
- Does short-term sacrifice enable long-term gain?

**Related:** Deferred gratification, compounding effects

---

### **Timing Risk**
**Definition:** The risk that entering a market too early (before it's ready) or too late (after it's crowded) reduces success probability

**When to apply:** Market entry decisions, product launch timing
**Key questions:**
- Is the market ready for this solution?
- Are competitors moving? How fast?
- What's the cost of being too early vs too late?

**Related:** First-mover vs fast-follower, market maturity curve

---

### **First-Mover vs Fast Follower**
**Definition:** First-movers capture early market share but face execution risk; fast-followers learn from pioneers' mistakes

**When to apply:** Market entry and competitive positioning
**Key questions:**
- Are there advantages to being first?
- Can we learn from others' attempts?
- Does this market have "winner-take-all" dynamics?

**Related:** Network effects, switching costs, learning curve advantages

---

## Systems Thinking Models

### **Feedback Loops**
**Definition:** How outputs of a system become inputs, creating reinforcement (positive) or stabilization (negative)

**When to apply:** Understanding strategic impact over time
**Key questions:**
- Does this option create positive reinforcement?
- What negative feedback loops will stabilize the system?
- Are there delayed effects we need to consider?

**Related:** Systems thinking, second-order effects, unintended consequences

---

### **Network Effects**
**Definition:** The value of a product or service increases as more people use it

**When to apply:** Platform and marketplace strategies
**Key questions:**
- Does this option strengthen network effects?
- What's the critical mass needed?
- How do we accelerate the flywheel?

**Related:** Platform thinking, winner-take-all dynamics, viral growth

---

### **Platform Dynamics**
**Definition:** Multi-sided markets where value creation depends on orchestrating interactions between different user groups

**When to apply:** Platform strategy, ecosystem development
**Key questions:**
- Which sides of the platform are we connecting?
- What's the core value exchange?
- How do we bootstrap initial liquidity?

**Related:** Network effects, two-sided markets, ecosystem strategy

---

### **Second-Order Effects**
**Definition:** The consequences of consequences—ripple effects that occur after initial impact

**When to apply:** Evaluating downstream strategic impact
**Key questions:**
- What happens after the immediate effect?
- What ripple effects might this create?
- Are there unintended consequences?

**Related:** Systems thinking, feedback loops, unintended consequences

---

## Decision Quality Models

### **One-Way vs Two-Way Doors**
**Definition:** One-way doors are irreversible decisions; two-way doors can be reversed if needed

**When to apply:** Deciding how much analysis to do before deciding
**Key questions:**
- Is this decision reversible?
- What's the cost of reversing?
- How much certainty do we need given reversibility?

**Related:** 70% rule, decision speed vs analysis depth

---

### **70% Rule**
**Definition:** If you have ~70% of the information you'd like to have, and you're ~70% confident, decide

**When to apply:** Avoiding analysis paralysis on two-way door decisions
**Key questions:**
- Do we have enough information to proceed?
- What's the cost of gathering more information?
- Can we course-correct if we're wrong?

**Related:** One-way vs two-way doors, satisficing vs optimizing

---

### **Regret Minimization**
**Definition:** Choose the option that minimizes potential future regret

**When to apply:** High-stakes decisions with emotional weight
**Key questions:**
- What would you regret a year from now?
- What's the downside if you're wrong?
- Which option has more acceptable worst-case outcome?

**Related:** Expected value, risk tolerance, loss aversion

---

### **Opportunity Cost**
**Definition:** The value of the next-best alternative you're giving up by choosing a specific option

**When to apply:** Resource allocation, prioritization decisions
**Key questions:**
- What are we NOT doing if we choose this?
- What's the value of the foregone alternative?
- Are we optimizing the right portfolio of investments?

**Related:** ROI, resource allocation, strategic trade-offs

---

## Strategy Models

### **Strategy Kernel** (Rumelt)
**Definition:** Good strategy = Diagnosis + Guiding Policy + Coherent Actions

**When to apply:** Evaluating strategic clarity and coherence
**Key questions:**
- What's the core challenge we're addressing? (Diagnosis)
- What's our overall approach? (Guiding Policy)
- What specific actions implement that approach? (Coherent Actions)

**Related:** Strategic clarity, bad strategy vs good strategy

---

### **Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)**
**Definition:** Customers hire products to make progress in their lives—the "job" is the progress they're trying to make

**When to apply:** Customer analysis, value proposition design
**Key questions:**
- What job is the customer trying to get done?
- What progress are they trying to make?
- What's currently frustrating about getting this job done?

**Related:** Customer outcomes, value proposition, innovation opportunities

---

### **Working Backwards** (Amazon)
**Definition:** Start from the ideal customer experience and work backwards to requirements

**When to apply:** Product strategy, feature prioritization
**Key questions:**
- What's the ideal press release?
- What's the ideal customer FAQ?
- What customer experience would make this remarkable?

**Related:** Customer-centricity, PR/FAQ framework, outcome-driven

---

### **Platform Thinking**
**Definition:** Design systems that enable others to create value by leveraging your core capabilities

**When to apply:** Product platform strategy, ecosystem development
**Key questions:**
- What core assets can others leverage?
- How do we enable value creation by others?
- What's the platform vs product split?

**Related:** Network effects, ecosystem strategy, plug-in economics

---

## Optimization Models

### **Local Maxima**
**Definition:** The tendency to optimize within current constraints rather than step back to find higher peaks

**When to apply:** Evaluating incremental vs transformative change
**Key questions:**
- Are we optimizing the current approach or do we need a bigger shift?
- What constraints are limiting our thinking?
- What would a "step back" reveal?

**Related:** First principles thinking, escape from local optima

---

### **Sunk Cost Fallacy**
**Definition:** The tendency to continue investing because of past investment rather than future value

**When to apply:** Portfolio decisions, continuing vs killing initiatives
**Key questions:**
- If we started fresh today, would we make this investment?
- Are we holding on because of what we've already spent?
- What's the forward-looking ROI?

**Related:** Opportunity cost, fresh-start thinking, zero-based budgeting

---

### **Confirmation Bias**
**Definition:** The tendency to seek information that confirms existing beliefs and discount disconfirming evidence

**When to apply:** Hypothesis testing, assumption validation
**Key questions:**
- What would prove us wrong?
- Are we seeking disconfirming evidence?
- What information are we avoiding?

**Related:** Falsification, intellectual honesty, devil's advocate

---

### **Survivorship Bias**
**Definition:** Concentrating on the people or things that survived past some process and overlooking those that didn't

**When to apply:** Learning from examples, case study analysis
**Key questions:**
- What failed that we're not seeing?
- Are we over-weighting visible successes?
- What's the graveyard of similar attempts?

**Related:** Selection bias, base rates, representative sample

---

## Competitive Models

### **Moat Analysis**
**Definition:** What sustainable competitive advantages protect the business from competition

**When to apply:** Competitive positioning, valuation assessment
**Key questions:**
- What defends our market position?
- How durable are these advantages?
- What moats could competitors build?

**Related:** Network effects, switching costs, brand, regulatory barriers

---

### **Switching Costs**
**Definition:** The costs (time, money, effort, risk) for customers to change from one solution to another

**When to apply:** Competitive positioning, retention strategy
**Key questions:**
- What would it take for customers to switch?
- How do we increase switching costs over time?
- What are competitors doing to reduce switching friction?

**Related:** Lock-in, integration, data accumulation, workflow

---

### **Zero-Sum vs Positive-Sum**
**Definition:** Zero-sum games have fixed value (one side's gain is other's loss); positive-sum games expand total value

**When to apply:** Competitive strategy, partnership evaluation
**Key questions:**
- Is this a competitive or collaborative scenario?
- Can we expand the pie vs fighting over slices?
- What partnerships create positive-sum outcomes?

**Related:** Win-win, ecosystem growth, market expansion

---

## Organizational Models

### **Principal-Agent Problem**
**Definition:** When incentives between decision-makers (principals) and executors (agents) are misaligned

**When to apply:** Organizational design, stakeholder alignment
**Key questions:**
- Are incentives aligned across stakeholders?
- What behaviors do current incentives drive?
- How do we align interests?

**Related:** Incentive design, stakeholder analysis, organizational structure

---

### **Coordination Cost**
**Definition:** The time, effort, and resources required to align multiple parties toward a common goal

**When to apply:** Organizational structure, process design
**Key questions:**
- What's the cost of coordination here?
- Can we reduce dependencies?
- What's the communication overhead?

**Related:** Conway's Law, team topology, Dunbar's number

---

### **Availability Bias**
**Definition:** Over-weighting information that's readily available (recent, salient, emotionally charged)

**When to apply:** Decision-making under information asymmetry
**Key questions:**
- What information are we missing?
- Are we over-weighting recent events?
- What's not visible that matters?

**Related:** Base rate neglect, salience bias, recency bias

---

## How to Use This Catalog

**During Strategic Thinking Sessions:**

1. **Assessment Phase:** Scan the catalog for models relevant to the decision context
2. **Selection:** Choose 3-5 models that provide different analytical lenses
3. **Application:** Apply each model to generate insights and test assumptions
4. **Synthesis:** Combine insights from multiple models to build robust strategic understanding

**Common Model Combinations:**

- **Business Model Evaluation:** Unit Economics + ROI + Time Horizon
- **Market Entry Decision:** Timing Risk + First-Mover vs Fast Follower + Network Effects
- **Platform Strategy:** Platform Dynamics + Network Effects + Switching Costs
- **Portfolio Decision:** Opportunity Cost + Local Maxima + Sunk Cost Fallacy
- **Risk Assessment:** Expected Value + Regret Minimization + One-Way vs Two-Way Doors
