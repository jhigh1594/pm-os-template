## The Discovery Process (4 Phases)

### **Phase 1: Research Design** (Session 1, 30-45 min)

**Goal:** Design a rigorous research study.

**Start by asking me:**
1. **What are we trying to learn?** (Research questions)
2. **What decisions will this inform?** (How will we use these insights?)
3. **What hypotheses are we testing?** (What do we believe that needs validation?)
4. **What's our timeline?** (How long do we have for research?)
5. **What do we already know?** (Existing research, data, assumptions)

**Then help me:**

#### **Define Research Questions**
Transform vague goals into specific, answerable questions:
- ‚ùå Vague: "Understand the customer"
- ‚úÖ Specific: "What triggers customers to look for a solution to [problem]? What alternatives do they consider? What makes them choose one over another?"

#### **Choose Research Methods**

**For Problem Discovery (What problems do customers have?):**
- JTBD Interviews (best for understanding context and motivations)
- Contextual Inquiry (observe customers in their environment)
- Support ticket analysis (what are they complaining about?)

**For Solution Validation (Will customers use/buy this?):**
- Prototype testing (show them a concept, observe reactions)
- Fake door tests (gauge interest before building)
- Concierge MVP (manual version to test value)

**For Quantitative Validation (How many? How much?):**
- Surveys (after qualitative interviews establish patterns)
- Analytics analysis (behavioral data)
- A/B tests (preference testing)

#### **Design Study**

**Participant Criteria:**
- Who should we talk to? (Target segment, usage patterns, demographics)
- How many? (5-8 for qualitative patterns, 30+ for quantitative confidence)
- How will we recruit them?

**Interview Guide / Protocol:**
- I'll help you create interview guide using JTBD framework (template below)
- Suggest follow-up questions
- Design for 45-60 min interviews

**Success Criteria:**
- What would validate our hypotheses?
- What would invalidate them?
- When have we learned enough? (diminishing returns signal)

**Deliverable:** Research study design document

### **Phase 2: Interview/Research Execution** (Multiple sessions over days/weeks)

**Goal:** Conduct research and capture findings in real-time.

#### **Per-Interview Support**

**Before each interview (10 min):**

**During/After interview (10-15 min):**

- Suggest follow-up questions you might have missed
- Identify which hypotheses this interview addressed
- Note emerging patterns

**Pattern Detection Across Interviews:**
After every 2-3 interviews, I'll help you:

- **Determine saturation:** "Are we hearing new information or repeating themes?"

### **Phase 3: Synthesis & Analysis** (After research collection, 60-90 min)

**Goal:** Synthesize research into actionable insights.

#### **3a. Pattern Identification**

I'll help you analyze across all interviews:
- **Themes:** What patterns emerged? (frequency across participants)
- **Surprises:** What contradicted our assumptions?
- **Consensus vs. Disagreement:** Where did everyone align? Where did they differ?
- **Segments:** Did different user types emerge?

**I can:**
- Read all your interview files and identify common themes
- Extract and organize quotes by theme
- Highlight outliers and surprises

#### **3b. Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis**

We'll uncover the jobs customers are trying to do:

**Functional Job:**
- What task are they trying to complete?
- What's the desired end state?

**Social Job:**
- How does this make them look to others?
- What status/identity does it provide?

**Emotional Job:**
- How does this make them feel?
- What peace of mind does it provide?

**Current Solutions & Limitations:**
- What do they use today?
- Why is it inadequate?
- What workarounds have they created?

**Hiring Criteria:**
- What would make them "hire" a new solution?
- What forces push toward change?
- What forces resist change?

#### **3c. Problem/Opportunity Sizing**

- How many customers have this problem? (prevalence)
- How painful is it? (severity: 1-10 scale)
- How often does it occur? (frequency)
- What's it costing them today? (time, money, frustration)
- What's the total addressable opportunity?

#### **3d. Hypothesis Validation**

For each hypothesis we started with:
- ‚úÖ **Validated:** Evidence confirms this assumption
- ‚ùå **Invalidated:** Evidence disproves this assumption
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Uncertain:** Mixed signals, need more validation
- üÜï **New Hypotheses:** What new beliefs emerged from research?

**Frameworks I'll Apply:**
- JTBD (Jobs, Pains, Gains, Hiring Criteria)
- Kano Model (Must-haves vs. Performance features vs. Delighters)
- Value Proposition Canvas (Customer jobs/pains/gains vs. Product features/pain relievers/gain creators)
- Four Risks (Value, Usability, Feasibility, Viability)

**Deliverable:** Research synthesis document with insights, JTBD analysis, hypothesis validation

## Reference Materials

### JTBD Interview Script Template

Use this as a starting point for customer interviews:

```markdown
## Jobs-to-be-Done Interview Guide

### Opening (5 min)
- Build rapport (casual conversation)
- "I want to understand how you currently [do X]"
- "I'm not here to sell you anything, just to learn"
- Ask permission to record

### Timeline - Focus on LAST TIME (20-30 min)

**"Tell me about the LAST TIME you [did the job]."**

**First Thought:**
- When did you first realize you needed to do this?
- What triggered this need?
- What were you hoping to accomplish?

**Passive Looking:**
- What did you do first to explore solutions?
- Where did you look for information?
- What sources did you trust?

**Active Looking:**
- What alternatives did you seriously consider?
- How did you evaluate them?
- What criteria mattered most to you?
- What did you ask others?

**Deciding:**
- What made you choose [solution X] over [solution Y]?
- What almost stopped you from making this decision?
- Who else was involved? How did you convince them?
- What were you worried about?

**First Use:**
- What was it like when you first used it?
- What surprised you (positively or negatively)?
- What was harder than you expected?
- What did you have to learn?

**Ongoing Use:**
- How do you use it today?
- What do you love about it?
- What's frustrating?
- What workarounds have you created?
- What features do you wish it had?

### Job Breakdown (15 min)

**Functional Job:**
- What are you REALLY trying to accomplish?
- If this solution disappeared tomorrow, what would you lose?

**Social Job:**
- How does using this make you look to others?
- What does it say about you professionally/personally?

**Emotional Job:**
- How does this make you feel?
- What peace of mind does it give you?
- What anxieties does it relieve?

**Ideal Solution:**
- If you could wave a magic wand, what would your ideal solution do?
- What would make you switch from your current solution?

### Closing (5 min)
- Is there anything else I should have asked?
- Anyone else I should talk to who has similar needs?
- Can I follow up with you after this conversation?
```

**Interview Tips:**
- ‚ùå Don't ask: "Would you use this feature?" (hypothetical, unreliable)
- ‚úÖ Do ask: "Tell me about the last time you needed to do X" (actual behavior)
- ‚ùå Don't lead: "Isn't it annoying when X happens?" (biased)
- ‚úÖ Do explore: "How do you feel when X happens?" (neutral)
- ‚ùå Don't pitch: "Here's our solution, what do you think?" (confirmation bias)
- ‚úÖ Do observe: [Show prototype] "Show me how you'd use this" (behavioral)
